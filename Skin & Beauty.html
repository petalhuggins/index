Skin & Beauty

The skin is one of the body's main organs. As a result, taking care of your skin has a strong impact on your overall wellbeing. Your skin serves as a defensive barrier and is the most exposed to the elements. It is influenced by a greater number of factors than you would expect. The following, for example, may have an effect on your overall skin health.

  • tanning bed exposure to UV radiation
  • Tobacco exposure to environmental contaminants long periods of time
  • unregulated sun exposure not having enough rest, fluids, or nutrition
  • aging

    • Taking care of your skin

    You should take precautions to ensure that your skin is safe. The following are some of them:

    1. Cleanse on a regular basis,usually twice a day.
    2. If you have oily skin, use a toner after cleansing.
    3. If you have dry skin, use a moisturizer.
    4. Remove dead skin cells and brighten the complexion by exfoliating

    Before using any skin care products please consider the following

  • For oily skin :Look for products with alpha hydroxy acids (glycolic acid or salicylic acid), benzoyl peroxide, and hyaluronic acid if you have oily skin. These ingredients help to manage excess sebum production, while hyaluronic acid hydrates only the areas that need it.
  • For dry skin: Look for shea butter and lactic acid in your products. To keep dry skin looking radiant, these ingredients include hydration and mild exfoliation.
  • For sensitive skin: Look for aloe vera, oatmeal, and shea butter in your products. They're effective moisturizers that rarely cause breakouts.
